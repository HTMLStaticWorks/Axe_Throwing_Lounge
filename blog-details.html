5 Secrets to Nailing the Bullseye Every Time

Coach Marcus June 15, 2026 5 min read

Ready to improve your target performance? Nail the bullseye consistently with these 5 essential parameters tracked by our range instruction team.

1. Keep Your Eyes on the Target

Many novice throwers lose target focus right during release. Avoid look shifts: pick one specific circular outline point on the bullseye target and stare at it until the axe has successfully embedded.

2. Use the Overhand Double-Handed Grip

For high accuracy, start with a double-handed grip. Fold your dominant hand over the bottom handle, then support it with the non-dominant hand. This aligns throwing balance and reduces horizontal shifts.

3. Find Your Stance Marker

Consistency is key. Choose a comfortable stance line marker. If the axe hits the target flat (handle first), you are too far back. If it lands blade top first, step back slightly.

4. Do Not Flick Your Wrist

This is the most common throwing mistake. Let the natural release rotation happen. Keep your wrist locked straight as you bring your arms forwards over your head.

5. Trust the Release Point

Release the handle just as your arms align parallel with the floor level. Releasing too early or too late will throw off the rotation angle.
